Nadi Pareeksha: Ayurvedic Technique Of Accurate Diagnosis

Nadi Pareeksha is also known as ‘Nadi chikitsa’ or the Ayurvedic pulse reading, which can accurately diagnose both physical and mental diseases as well as dosha imbalances. It is an important diagnostic tool used by Ayurvedic doctors and consists of various examination or physical tests. Nadi Pareeksha is widely used since 5000 years for the diagnosis of imbalance of dosha in the body and concluding a disease.

Garbhasanskar:

The Sanskrit word “Garbh” means a foetus in the womb and “Sanskar” means educating the mind. So, Garbhasanskar essentially means educating the mind of the foetus.
Things covered in Garbhasanskar are:
1.Assessment of current health status
2.Creating awareness and explaining the importance of garbhasanskar
3.Knowledge about month wise foetal development
4.Month wise diet plan according to Ayurveda
5.Trimester wise yoga and pranayama
6.Meditation and its importance

Panchakarma

Panchakarma treatment in Ayurveda refers to five therapeutic measures (Karmas) to be undertaken for complete Detoxification of body. The Panchakarma treatment includes five Ayurvedic therapies:
1.Vaman (Medicated Emesis)
2.Virechan (Purgation)
3.Nasyam (Nasal Instillation)
4.Kashay Basti (Enema)
5.Sneha Basti (Enema)

Yoga

Yoga is the ancient practice of mind and body that combines physical postures, breathing techniques and meditation.
The practice finds its origins in India, dating back to 3000 BC and is believed to have been developed by the sages of the Indus Saraswati Valley civilization.
Yoga is a mind and body practice. Various styles of yoga combine physical postures, breathing techniques, and meditation or relaxation. Yoga is an ancient practice that may have originated in India. It involves movement, meditation, and breathing techniques to promote mental and physical well-being.

Diet

The Ayurvedic diet is an eating pattern that’s been around for thousands of years. It’s based on the principles of Ayurvedic medicine and focuses on balancing different types of energy within your body, which is said to improve health.
The Ayurvedic diet is a component of Ayurveda and has been practiced for thousands of years. It’s based on determining your dominant dosha and eating specific foods to promote balance between all three doshas.
Given that the Ayurvedic diet emphasizes nutrient-rich whole foods, it might boost weight loss. While limited research is available on the Ayurvedic diet and weight loss, some studies have found that it may be effective in this regard.

Mental Health

Mental health includes our emotional, behavioural and social well-being. To have a good physical health it is very important that one has normal and healthy mind. However, the stressful and hectic modern lifestyle increases risk of having poor Mental Health. Mental illnesses such as depression, anxiety or sleep problems are seen very commonly among people who have a very unhealthy lifestyle that includes poor diet, lack of physical activity, lack of social engagement and too much stress. It is a well-known fact that mental stress can eventually have some serious effects on our physical health.
